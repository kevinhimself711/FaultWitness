# I-0011 — Durable State, Checkpoint and Transactional Events

## Status

Planned; depends on I-0009 and I-0010.

## Objective

Make owner state, idempotency, checkpoint, and at-least-once delivery recoverable under duplicates, crashes, and stale workers.

## Scope

- Owner-isolated PostgreSQL roles/schemas, Alembic migrations, optimistic versions, idempotency records, Outbox, Inbox, and DLQ.
- Redis Streams publisher/consumer with claim recovery, deduplication, backoff, backlog readiness, and poison-message semantics.
- Fenced encrypted PostgreSQL checkpoint with lease validation and atomic graph Event/Outbox writes.

## Non-goals

No distributed exactly-once claim, external action execution, or G06 throughput/scaling certification.

## Test and Eval

EVAL-G01-005 runs all 82 transitions against real PostgreSQL, 10,000 duplicate deliveries, 100 crash points, stale-fence races, migration drift, and backlog/DLQ faults.

## Pass criteria

- Partial state/outbox commit, duplicate state mutation, stale-fence acceptance, and committed checkpoint loss: 0.
- Every transition conforms on real PostgreSQL.
- Duplicate/crash matrices converge without repeated committed effect.
- Candidate closes with migration drift, unexplained DLQ, and readiness backlog: 0.
